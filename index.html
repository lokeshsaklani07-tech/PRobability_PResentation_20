1. Before Probability: The Foundation

To understand chance, we first need to understand how we group outcomes. Enter Sets, Relations, & Functions.

Sets

A collection of distinct, well-defined objects. (e.g., A deck of 52 cards)

Relations & Functions

Relation: Connection between elements of two sets.
Function: A special relation where every input has exactly ONE output.

2. Union & Intersection

The grammar of Sets

∪

Union (OR)

Everything in Set A OR Set B (or both).
"I'll take tea or coffee."

∩

Intersection (AND)

Only what's common in Set A AND Set B.
"I only want ginger tea."

3. What is Probability?

P(Event) = Favorable Outcomes / Total Outcomes
  • It's the math of measuring uncertainty.
  • Always a value between 0 (Impossible) and 1 (Certain).
  • If P(E) = 0.5, you have a 50/50 shot.

4. Sample Space: The ₹1 Toss

The classic Indian coin flip

The Sample Space (S) is the collection of ALL possible outcomes.

Tossing a single Indian 1 Rupee coin:

S = { Heads, Tails }

Total elements = 2

Probability of getting Heads = 1/2 (50%)

5. Sample Space: 2 Dice Roll

Rolling snake eyes 🎲🎲

When you roll two standard dice, the sample space expands exponentially!

  • Die 1 has 6 outcomes, Die 2 has 6 outcomes.
  • Total outcomes = 6 × 6 = 36 possible combinations!
S = {(1,1), (1,2) ... (3,4) ... (6,6)}

6. Sample Space: 3 Coins Toss

Flipping HHH to TTT

  • Coin 1: 2 outcomes
  • Coin 2: 2 outcomes
  • Coin 3: 2 outcomes
  • Total = 2 × 2 × 2 = 8 outcomes

The Full Space (S)

{HHH, HHT, HTH, HTT,
THH, THT, TTH, TTT}

7. Basic Theorem: Addition Rule

Probability of A OR B happening

P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)

Why subtract P(A ∩ B)?
Because if we just add A and B, we count the overlapping area TWICE! We must subtract the overlap to secure an accurate probability.

8. Basic Theorem: Multiplication Rule

Probability of A AND B happening

P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B | A)
  • If events are Independent (e.g. coin tosses), it simplifies to:
    P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B)
  • Example: Flipping two heads in a row is (1/2) × (1/2) = 1/4 or 25%.

9. The Legendary Bayes' Theorem

Updating beliefs based on new evidence

P(A | B) = [ P(B | A) × P(A) ] / P(B)

ELI5: What is the probability I have a disease (A) GIVEN THAT my test result is positive (B)?

Bayes theorem revolutionized machine learning algorithm structures!
